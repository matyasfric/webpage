The Impact of Two-Week Social Media Detox on Selected University Students from Prague: A Case Study

Objective

To investigate the effects of a social media detox on participants’ mental health, productivity, daily schedule/time management, and social interactions on selected university students from Prague.

Methodology

  1. Participant Selection:
    • Recruit a group of 10 participants from universities in Prague.
  2. Pre-Detox Assessment:
    • Conduct surveys and interviews to assess participants’ current mental health, productivity levels, social interactions, background (like workload with the studium, uni-work-life balance), set up starting point (screen time, social media usage).
    • Use standardized tools like the DASS-21 (Depression, Anxiety, and Stress Scale), EQ-5D measure or other established measures (questionnaires) to measurably evaluate pre-detox starting conditions.
    • Setting and defining clear initial rules that must be followed for the proper conduct of the case study.
  3. Detox Period:
    • Participants will abstain from all social media platforms for 2 weeks.
    • Support of other team members, giving full and truthful information, including any potential breaks in abstinence.
  4. During Detox:
    • Regular check-ins with participants to monitor participants’ progress and address any challenges.
    • Encourage participants to keep a journal documenting their experiences, taking any notes, feelings, and any changes they notice.
    • Sticking to agreed pre-detox conditions.
  5. Post-Detox Assessment:
    • Repeat the initial questionnaires and interviews to measure changes in mental health, productivity, social interactions and quality of life.
    • Comparison of pre-detox and post-detox statuses and the data, including notes taken during the detox.
    • Identifying changes.

Data Analysis

  • Comparison of pre-detox and post-detox.
  • Look for patterns and correlations between social media usage and changes in mental health and productivity.

Expected Outcomes

  • Insights into how social media affects mental health and productivity of university students.
  • Recommendations for healthier social media usage habits.
  • Potential long-term benefits of regular social media detoxes.

Challenges and Considerations

  • Ensuring participant adherence to the detox period.
  • Addressing potential withdrawal symptoms or increased anxiety due to lack of social media.
  • Ethical considerations in monitoring participants’ mental health.

Conclusion

Summarize the findings and discuss their implications for individuals and society. Highlight any limitations of the study and suggest areas for future research. To analyze the impact on life of university students in Prague.
